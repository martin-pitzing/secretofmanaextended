# Dyluck Mission Brief

## Purpose

This brief closes one of the main open narrative gaps: what Dyluck was actually sent to do, who authorized it, and why the mission matters politically beyond romance.

Use these labels:

- `Original anchor`: present in *Secret of Mana*
- `Added revisit change`: new interpretation for this project

## Original Anchor

- Dyluck disappears on a mission tied to Pandora's crisis.
- His absence motivates Primm and shapes early Pandora urgency.

## Added Revisit Change

- Dyluck's mission was not a clean military action.
- It was a politically split operation with three competing purposes:
  - a public rescue and reconnaissance assignment
  - a private court test of deniability and expendability
  - a hidden search for evidence connecting Pandora's corruption to temple and imperial movement

## Mission Name

- internal military label: `North Route Stabilization Survey`
- court label used publicly: `Border Relief and Escort Mission`
- hidden administrative note: `limited-risk deniable recovery operation`

## Who Authorized It

### Formal Authorization

- King Regan signed the final order.
- Marshal Tiber Halwen backed the mission on strategic grounds.

### Political Pressure Behind It

- Chancellor Maelin Voss allowed the mission to proceed because Dyluck's removal reduced internal pressure on the court.
- Lady Seremne opposed the operation in its final form because it combined public necessity with private expendability.

## What The Public Was Told

- Dyluck was escorting relief movement and scouting hostile activity near a corrupted route.
- The mission was framed as urgent, honorable, and temporary.
- Publicly, it looked like the kind of task only Dyluck could manage because he was trusted by both soldiers and civilians.

## What The Real Operational Goal Was

- verify reports of a ruined corridor linked to both temple disturbance and irregular magical activity
- recover any surviving field testimony before the Empire or local panic could shape the story first
- determine whether the corruption near Pandora had external coordination rather than local origin

## What The Court Quietly Expected

- If Dyluck succeeded, Pandora gained proof and a heroic symbol.
- If Dyluck failed or vanished, the court lost a destabilizing internal voice while preserving a martyr narrative.
- This is the moral stain at the center of the mission.

## Where The Mission Went Wrong

- The route was already compromised by coordinated ritual interference.
- Field signs suggested that the corruption pattern was not random monster spread.
- Dyluck's unit was drawn toward a controlled false lead that combined necromantic manipulation with knowledge of local military habits.
- This is where Thanatos's network first intersects with Pandora's political failure.

## What Dyluck Learned Before Disappearance

- someone had been feeding selective route intelligence into Pandora's command chain
- the corruption had a ritual structure
- at least one local sacred site or ruined ward-line had been studied by outside actors
- the mission had likely been planned with an acceptable casualty expectation higher than he was told

## What Survived

- partial route markings
- a damaged ledger fragment
- one unfinished field note in Dyluck's own hand
- survivor rumor that he chose to split his group to get civilians or weaker soldiers out

## Why This Matters For Primm

- It changes Dyluck from "missing lover" into evidence of how Pandora consumes its best people.
- It gives Primm a reason to attack the court system itself, not only the people keeping her from him.
- It makes later encounters with false hope, illusion, or possession much more painful because the original betrayal was political before it became supernatural.

## Why This Matters For The Empire

- Kroll can exploit the mission as proof Pandora cannot govern its own crisis.
- Rourke can read it as exactly the kind of weak chain-of-command failure the Empire claims to solve.
- Vey can use the route data as evidence that sacred corruption follows infrastructure patterns.

## Why This Matters For Chapter Structure

- Chapter 2 now has a concrete investigative center.
- Chapter 3 can inherit the route fragments into Gaia's Navel and deeper ruin history.
- Later Primm scenes can argue from proof, not only emotion.

## Recommended Follow-Up Uses

- feed Dyluck's field note into one later memory artifact
- use the mission route as a connective spine between Pandora, haunted corridors, and temple destabilization
- let at least two factions tell different versions of what Dyluck died or vanished for
