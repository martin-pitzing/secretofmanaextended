# Subculture Briefs

## Purpose

These briefs convert the broader world-texture pass into scene-ready, reusable groups.

They should appear in towns, travel routes, temple margins, rumor networks, and side quests.

## 1. Salvage Caravans

- role: relic movers, rumor traders, practical historians of broken infrastructure
- original anchor: dwarves and ruins already imply a salvage economy in *Secret of Mana*
- added revisit change: mixed caravans now act as mobile connectors between temple damage, trade routes, and imperial interest
- social use: merchants distrust them, dwarves half-respect them, the Empire monitors them
- quest use: they can sell fragments, maps, or false leads, and they may know where missing route ledgers resurfaced
- visual direction: patched wagons, suspended relic cages, tagged crates, repurposed shrine cloth as weather cover

## 2. Shrine Gardeners

- role: plant-attuned caretakers who preserve neglected sacred growth and minor ward-sites
- added series input: evokes the stranger, gentler side of the Mana universe without importing heavy canon directly
- added revisit change: they know seasonal rites, root illnesses, and shrine decay patterns better than palace authorities do
- social use: rural communities trust them more than distant temple archives
- quest use: can diagnose ecological mana imbalance before magic specialists do
- visual direction: reed tools, woven bark satchels, pollen veils, root-mark gloves

## 3. Moon-Touched Clans

- role: upland or night-route families preserving grief customs, dream taboos, and moon-route navigation
- added revisit change: they broaden the sacred world beyond court and temple centers
- social use: sometimes treated as wise, sometimes treated as unsettling
- quest use: ideal foreshadowing for Moon Palace material, dream corruption, and altered memory
- visual direction: layered cold-weather cloth, reflective beadwork, crescent route charms, sleep-mark pigments

## 4. Archive Couriers

- role: trusted route runners who move sealed records between palace, shrine, and court
- original anchor: implied by temple archives and court records, but not foregrounded
- added revisit change: they become crucial because control of documents is now one of the main forms of power in the setting
- social use: respected, bribed, threatened, or quietly recruited by every major faction
- quest use: can carry truth, forgeries, panic, or proof across chapter boundaries
- visual direction: wax-case satchels, coded ribbons, route knives, waterproof document tubes

## Best Early Uses

- use salvage caravans near Gaia's Navel and Earth Vault routes
- use shrine gardeners around Potos, river shrines, and the Water Palace outskirts
- use moon-touched clans in foreshadowing, dreams, and highland side routes before the Moon Palace
- use archive couriers in Pandora, Water Palace, and any chapter where legitimacy or truth is contested
