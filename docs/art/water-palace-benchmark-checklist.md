# Water Palace Benchmark Production Checklist

## Purpose

This is the execution-ready checklist for the first environment benchmark.

It turns the already locked benchmark choice into a production task list that can be handed directly to environment art, UI/portrait, and VFX work.

## Benchmark Scope

- primary room: `Water Palace inner chamber`
- supporting micro-space: `short exterior causeway or palace approach`
- scene target: Luka's first major audience and ritual interaction
- no combat required in benchmark v1

## Success Criteria

- the room reads clearly at gameplay camera distance
- the scene supports dialogue blocking for at least `Randi`, `Luka`, and one attendant or `Archivist Neral`
- water, archive, and sacred architecture all read as distinct functions
- the palette feels sacred and calm without flattening detail
- one ritual VFX beat proves the Mana lighting language

## Environment Task List

### Tile And Surface Set

- main floor tiles for pale stone with worn sacred geometry
- water-channel edge tiles
- raised dais or audience platform tiles
- stair or step transition tiles
- wall and pillar tiles for chamber framing
- channel inlay variations for visual hierarchy

### Prop Set

- ritual basin
- suspended braziers
- script stands
- archive shelves or sealed record cases
- attendant markers or floor mats
- ceremonial vessels
- small shrine lighting fixtures

### Architectural Features

- one central focal axis toward Luka's position
- one secondary read toward the archive or sealed records
- one optional side conversation pocket for Neral or an attendant
- readable entry point and audience path

## Layout Checklist

- room supports direct entrance, short approach, and final audience stop
- player path does not obscure the ritual basin
- portrait dialogue staging can happen without hiding the most important room props
- there is one clean composition for:
  - entrance reveal
  - audience framing
  - ritual activation moment

## Lighting Checklist

- establish cool sacred base light
- add reflected water shimmer selectively, not across every surface
- separate archive-side light from ritual-side light subtly
- keep values readable for portraits and gameplay sprites
- avoid excessive bloom or fog that softens pixel edges

## VFX Checklist

- ambient drifting droplets or particle shimmer
- subtle channel glow along sacred inlays
- one ritual activation pulse from basin or central focus point
- one reflected-light response on nearby surfaces during activation
- no heavy combat spell effects in this benchmark pass

## Portrait Checklist

### First Locked Portrait Batch

- Randi
- Primm
- Luka
- King Regan
- Chancellor Voss
- Magistrate Severin Kroll

### Expression Count

For benchmark testing, each portrait should have at least:

- neutral
- determined
- strained
- vulnerable

Final production counts follow `docs/art/portrait-batch-01-spec.md`.

### Benchmark Scene Portrait Minimum

For the Water Palace scene specifically, prioritize:

- Randi
- Luka
- Archivist Neral

Optional fourth:

- one attendant portrait if needed for staging polish

## Sprite And Blocking Checklist

- Randi gameplay sprite
- Luka in-room authority sprite
- Archivist Neral or attendant support sprite
- clear standing positions for audience triangle blocking
- no more than one extra ambient NPC in the benchmark version

## Audio And Atmosphere Checklist

- soft water ambience
- low ceremonial room tone
- one ritual activation accent
- no layered noise that competes with dialogue readability

## Deliverable Order

1. grayscale layout blockout
2. palette and material pass
3. prop placement pass
4. lighting pass
5. ritual VFX pass
6. dialogue staging verification
7. portrait insertion test for Luka, Randi, and Neral

## Done Definition

The benchmark is done when:

- the room can support the Chapter 1 Water Palace audience scene
- portraits and gameplay sprites both read cleanly in-context
- the room proves the project's sacred-pixel visual lane better than a mockup alone
- the result is strong enough to become the reference room for later temple interiors
