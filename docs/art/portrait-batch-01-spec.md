# Portrait Batch 01 Spec

## Purpose

This document locks the first real dialogue-portrait production batch so character art can start without re-litigating scope.

This batch is sized to cover:

- the Water Palace benchmark scene
- Pandora political scenes
- the first six chapters of party and authority drama
- the first direct imperial and temple-order pressures

## Locked Portrait Rules

- format: `96x96` detailed pixel portrait panels
- style: crisp hand-authored pixel busts with selective subpixel softening only where facial readability benefits
- framing: chest-up or shoulders-up, default `3/4` angle
- lighting: regional palette influence, but never so strong that portraits lose character identity across scenes
- material priority: face, hair silhouette, neckline, fabric trim, and one iconic accessory must all read at a glance

## Expression Tiers

### Tier A: Playable Core Cast

Use `8` expressions each.

Required set:

- neutral
- determined
- strained
- vulnerable
- anger
- shock
- warmth or relief
- casting focus

### Tier B: Major Recurring Authorities And Political Figures

Use `6` expressions each.

Required set:

- neutral
- stern or determined
- strained
- private vulnerability
- anger
- shock or alarm

### Tier C: Secondary Recurring Authorities

Use `4` expressions each.

Required set:

- neutral
- concern
- strain
- severe or disapproving

## Pixel Style Defaults

- no painterly smudge treatment
- no anti-aliased remake gloss
- outline handling should be selective, with dark local-color edges instead of full black contour everywhere
- authority figures should carry readable trim hierarchy
- travelers should show wear, dust, repair, and climate impact
- expressions should push brow, mouth, and eye-shape first before adding effect overlays

## Character-Specific Notes

### Randi

- earthy palette base
- direct gaze should remain rare; he reads better as earnest than declarative early on
- sword-bearing identity should be implied by posture, not literal blade-in-frame in every portrait

### Primm

- noble tailoring must remain visible even in travel states
- emotional range should feel quicker and sharper than Randi's
- vulnerability expressions should not read passive

### Popoi

- asymmetry is essential
- expression set should feel less socially conventional than the human cast
- casting focus should look uncanny, not merely intense

### Luka

- calm authority first, institutional fatigue second
- her vulnerable expression should feel like withheld truth, not physical weakness

### Voss

- severity and control matter more than theatrical villainy
- private-vulnerability frame should suggest fear of collapse, not remorse

### Kroll

- his neutral should already feel invasive
- anger should stay restrained; his threat works better when he rarely looks overtly emotional

## Done Definition

Portrait Batch 01 is locked when:

- the cast list above is accepted as the initial portrait production scope
- expression counts are not treated as negotiable per artist pass
- the Water Palace, Pandora, and early temple cycle can all be staged without adding emergency portrait requests
