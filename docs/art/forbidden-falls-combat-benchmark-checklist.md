# Forbidden Falls Combat Benchmark Checklist

## Purpose

This document locks the first Chapter 1 combat benchmark beyond the exile road.

It complements the Water Palace inner chamber by proving the opposite half of the project's identity:

- sacred danger
- early melee readability
- environmental instability
- seal-break spectacle without overproduced effects

## Locked Choice

- benchmark combat room: `Forbidden Falls seal breach arena`
- chapter scene anchor: `ch01_sc02_forbidden_falls`
- role: secondary benchmark after the Water Palace inner chamber

## Why This Room

- it is the earliest scene where the game must prove combat, atmosphere, and story pressure at the same time
- it tests sacred danger rather than generic roadside combat
- it lets the sword pull, seal rupture, and first monster escalation share one contained space
- it is more visually specific than an anonymous forest lane and more narratively charged than a neutral training room

## What The Benchmark Must Prove

- player and enemy readability in a dense natural-sacred space
- readable hero locomotion near water edges and altar approach lanes
- early melee spacing around environmental edges and a central focal point
- clear attack commitment, enemy anticipation, and enemy recovery at early-game speeds
- seal-break VFX that feel dangerous and pixel-authored rather than glossy
- environmental storytelling before, during, and after a fight
- clean transition from exploration to combat to sword-pull moment

## Room Scope

- waterfall-edge arena
- one altar or seal-stone focal structure
- one combat approach lane
- one small jump, broken-footing, or landing read that proves traversal belongs in the action vocabulary without turning the room into a platform gauntlet
- one hazard edge or knockback-danger zone
- one post-fight visual state change showing the seal destabilized

## Layout Checklist

- player route into the room should telegraph "forbidden site" before the fight starts
- the altar must remain readable during combat
- waterfall or ravine edge should add danger without causing unfair camera clutter
- enemy approach vectors should be legible within one screen read
- the room should support a clear before/after composition:
  - sealed and tense
  - ruptured and unstable

## Combat Checklist

- one destabilized monster family with readable telegraphs
- melee hit-confirm readability at early-game weapon speeds
- clear punish windows after enemy commitment
- enough open floor space for first danger teaching
- at least one environmental pressure element:
  - slippery edge
  - broken footing
  - rushing water knockback threat

## VFX Checklist

- pre-fight seal shimmer
- rupture pulse on sword contact or scripted breach
- water spray or mist reaction during destabilization
- corrupted-mana flare from the monster emergence
- restrained screen feedback that preserves pixel edge clarity

## Tile And Prop Checklist

- wet stone floor tiles
- moss variation tiles
- altar or seal-stone set
- rope or warning-marker remnants
- spray-hit cliff tiles
- disturbed debris after rupture

## Animation Checklist

- first monster emergence or leap
- sword pull or contact beat
- altar crack or seal pulse
- one attack startup and recovery read that remains clear at gameplay distance
- one knockback or stagger animation readable in tight space

## Audio And Mood Checklist

- heavy waterfall bed
- sudden pressure-drop or resonance accent before the breach
- one rupture sting
- combat audio that remains readable under constant water ambience

## Deliverable Order

1. grayscale room blockout
2. combat metric pass
3. altar and edge readability pass
4. tile and prop pass
5. enemy placement and telegraph pass
6. rupture VFX pass
7. before/after state verification

## Done Definition

The benchmark is done when:

- Chapter 1 has one combat room strong enough to stand beside the Water Palace as a visual and gameplay reference
- the seal-breach fight reads as sacred crisis instead of generic field combat
- the room can serve as the baseline for later temple-rupture or relic-site combat spaces
