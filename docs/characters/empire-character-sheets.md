# Empire Character Sheets

## Scope

These sheets make the Empire legible as a coalition of power centers instead of a flat villain force.

## Character: Emperor Vandole

- ID: `char_emperor_vandole`
- Role: sovereign center of imperial reunification
- Faction ties: `fac_empire_court`, umbrella authority over all imperial blocs
- Public persona: visionary ruler promising peace through total order
- Internal motive: prove the world can still be governed before it collapses into regional failure and spiritual disorder
- Fear: inheriting decline too late to reverse it
- Contradiction: seeks salvation through coercion that reproduces the ruin he fears
- Relationship map: empowers Kroll politically, relies on Rourke materially, tolerates Vey instrumentally, is spiritually mirrored by Thorne
- Act function: apex expression of order without humility
- Faction leverage: law, spectacle, conquest authority, relic possession claims
- Visual design cues: ceremonial authority fused with militarized state iconography, severe vertical silhouette, relic-adjacent regalia

## Character: Magistrate Severin Kroll

- ID: `char_severin_kroll`
- Role: court adviser and political operator
- Faction ties: `fac_empire_court`
- Public persona: cultured envoy of lawful integration
- Internal motive: turn victory into consent by controlling symbols and succession optics
- Fear: military conquest without narrative legitimacy
- Contradiction: values order but thrives on covert destabilization
- Relationship map: speaks for the Emperor in delicate theaters, manipulates provincial elites, underestimates field resentment
- Act function: human face of imperial soft capture
- Faction leverage: diplomacy, blackmail, recognition, court access
- Visual design cues: immaculate dark formalwear, insignia used sparingly but precisely, urbane silhouette

## Character: General Cassian Rourke

- ID: `char_cassian_rourke`
- Role: Expansion Command field general
- Faction ties: `fac_empire_expansion_command`
- Public persona: blunt professional who delivers roads, order, and fear in equal measure
- Internal motive: secure the world physically before theorists destroy campaign viability
- Fear: ideological excess and magical obsession weakening real control
- Contradiction: can recognize civilian needs while enforcing brutal occupation
- Relationship map: clashes with Kroll over optics, tolerates Vey when useful, views Thorne as a destabilizer in uniformed language
- Act function: makes the Empire feel materially competent and dangerous
- Faction leverage: garrisons, supply lines, checkpoints, transport seizures
- Visual design cues: disciplined war gear, heavy traveling coat, practical insignia, broad campaign silhouette

## Character: Director Elra Vey

- ID: `char_elra_vey`
- Role: Mana Engineering Bureau director
- Faction ties: `fac_empire_mana_engineering_bureau`
- Public persona: precise scholar of lost systems serving imperial restoration
- Internal motive: understand and master ancient mana architecture regardless of which regime benefits
- Fear: ignorance preserved as morality
- Contradiction: scientific clarity paired with catastrophic ethical blindness
- Relationship map: useful to Vandole, frustrating to Rourke, intellectually aligned with but not loyal to Kroll, opportunistically adjacent to Thanatos-driven phenomena
- Act function: connects temples, corruption, and the Mana Fortress into one technical program
- Faction leverage: labs, relic studies, imperial research access, technical briefs
- Visual design cues: layered coat with instrument harnesses, luminous measuring devices, refined but hazardous silhouette

## Character: Preceptor Malrec Thorne

- ID: `char_malrec_thorne`
- Role: Unification Doctrine ideologue
- Faction ties: `fac_empire_unification_doctrine`
- Public persona: preacher-philosopher of necessary unity
- Internal motive: erase the plural loyalties he believes caused prior civilizational collapse
- Fear: mercy toward regional identity
- Contradiction: speaks of peace while sanctifying cultural erasure
- Relationship map: useful to Vandole as a mobilizer, irritating to Rourke, spiritually useful to Kroll, philosophically dangerous to everyone else
- Act function: gives the Empire a coherent worldview instead of only appetite
- Faction leverage: speeches, cadre formation, moral justification for severe policy
- Visual design cues: austere robes blended with state iconography, ritual severity, charismatic but hard silhouette

## Empire To Pandora Relationship Notes

- Kroll sees Pandora as a target for negotiated submission through elite fracture.
- Rourke sees Pandora as a border problem that becomes more expensive the longer court politics delay resolution.
- Vey sees Pandora as useful only insofar as access routes, archives, and temple adjacency matter.
- Thorne treats Pandora as proof that dynastic pluralism breeds weakness.
- This gives the Empire four different reasons to engage Pandora, which should surface in different scenes and documents.
