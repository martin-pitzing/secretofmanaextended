# Pandora Character Sheets

## Scope

These sheets turn Pandora from a generic kingdom in distress into a court with visible internal pressure.

## Character: King Regan of Pandora

- ID: `char_king_regan`
- Role: ruling monarch under siege pressure
- Faction ties: `fac_pandora_court`
- Public persona: stern protector trying to keep the kingdom from imploding
- Internal motive: preserve Pandora's continuity long enough to avoid annexation or internal fragmentation
- Fear: that his line will survive only by becoming morally hollow
- Contradiction: he loves his daughter, but treats her as leverage
- Relationship map: father to Primm, nominal superior to Dyluck, reliant on Chancellor Voss, increasingly argued with by Lady Seremne
- Act function: represents the court's instinct to preserve order through compromise
- Faction leverage: marriage, decrees, officer appointments, access to sacred and civic spaces
- Visual design cues: heavy war cloak over ceremonial dress, sleep-deprived eyes, rings and seals worn like duty not vanity

## Character: Captain Dyluck

- ID: `char_dyluck`
- Role: field commander and symbolic hero of Pandora
- Faction ties: `fac_pandora_court`, military wing of Pandora
- Public persona: composed, dependable, deeply respected by soldiers and commoners
- Internal motive: defend people without becoming the court's martyr-symbol
- Fear: that loyalty will be weaponized against those he loves
- Contradiction: obedient officer who increasingly doubts the system he protects
- Relationship map: beloved by Primm, useful to Regan, mistrusted by Voss because he cannot be fully controlled
- Act function: emotional and political hinge for Pandora's collapse arc
- Faction leverage: soldier trust, public morale, border credibility
- Visual design cues: practical armor with court insignia worn down by active service, open posture, commander silhouette that reads heroic before tragic

## Character: Chancellor Maelin Voss

- ID: `char_maelin_voss`
- Role: conservative court operator
- Faction ties: `fac_pandora_court`
- Public persona: calm administrator speaking the language of stability
- Internal motive: keep the court intact even if it means surrendering moral ground
- Fear: uncontrolled change more than foreign conquest
- Contradiction: calls himself pragmatic while making panic worse through secrecy
- Relationship map: advises Regan, opposes Primm's autonomy, quietly tests imperial contacts
- Act function: personifies the self-protective logic of declining institutions
- Faction leverage: records, emissaries, marriage arrangements, access control
- Visual design cues: immaculate layered robes, severe posture, elegant but cold silhouette

## Character: Lady Seremne Vale

- ID: `char_seremne_vale`
- Role: reform-minded noble voice
- Faction ties: `fac_pandora_court`
- Public persona: composed patron of relief and civic order
- Internal motive: force the court to acknowledge the cost borne by its own people
- Fear: that reform will come too late to matter
- Contradiction: compassionate in goal, capable of ruthless exposure when necessary
- Relationship map: ally to Primm, critic of Voss, wary supporter of Regan when crisis intensifies
- Act function: opens a path for Pandora to evolve rather than only collapse
- Faction leverage: noble network, civilian aid channels, moral credibility
- Visual design cues: refined but practical attire with public-service rather than court-display emphasis

## Character: Marshal Tiber Halwen

- ID: `char_tiber_halwen`
- Role: military hardliner attached to the border crisis
- Faction ties: `fac_pandora_court`
- Public persona: direct and disciplined commander who says what frightened nobles avoid saying
- Internal motive: turn Pandora into a survivable wartime state
- Fear: softness and divided command leading to annihilation
- Contradiction: protects the kingdom by making it harsher to live in
- Relationship map: useful to Regan, scornful of Voss, skeptical of Primm, professionally respects Dyluck
- Act function: keeps the war visible as policy, not just backdrop
- Faction leverage: troop movement, fortification decisions, emergency rule pressure
- Visual design cues: scarred field armor, limited ornament, square rigid silhouette, practical cloak and campaign map gear

## Pandora To Empire Relationship Notes

- Regan fears imperial conquest, but Voss is willing to normalize incremental concessions if they preserve court continuity.
- Dyluck and Halwen both oppose imperial control, but Dyluck still believes legitimacy matters while Halwen prioritizes survival mechanics.
- Seremne reads the Empire as both foreign threat and warning about what Pandora becomes if it sacrifices its people for stability.
- Primm's rebellion should grow inside this exact pressure system, not outside it.
