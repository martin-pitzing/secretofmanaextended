# Mana Core Character Sheets

## Scope

These sheets define the core cast for the revisit using the existing character-sheet style, while marking where the story departs from original *Secret of Mana* and where it borrows broader series logic from the Mana lineage.

## Character: Randi

- ID: `char_randi`
- Role: chosen bearer of the Mana Sword and the human-scale anchor of the story
- Faction ties: `fac_gemma_lineage`, `fac_potos_village`, temporary alliance with `fac_water_palace_order`
- Public persona: impulsive village boy thrust into mythic responsibility
- Internal motive: prove that he can protect people without becoming a weapon pointed by older powers
- Fear: that his worth only exists when he is useful to prophecy
- Contradiction: he is the strongest symbol of destiny in the party, but begins as the least socially prepared for the world he must cross
- Relationship map: son of Potos, protected and resented by village elders, guided by Luka, morally tested by temple authority, defined in contrast to the Empire’s system-building
- Act function: turns the adventure from local crisis into a world-spanning moral journey
- Faction leverage: the Mana Sword, Gemma tradition, inherited legitimacy, direct access to ancient sites
- Visual design cues: practical village clothing with mythic escalation over time, should read ordinary before becoming iconic
- Original Secret of Mana: a simple chosen hero with a clear heroic outline
- Revisit change: make his burden feel social and political, not just fated; Potos should have failed duties and he should carry the consequences of that failure
- Series input: treat him as a classic Mana-style wandering chosen hero, but less isolated from regional politics than in the original
- Temple implication: every temple should challenge his right to continue, not merely confirm it

## Character: Primm

- ID: `char_primm`
- Role: royal daughter, magician, and emotional counterweight to Randi
- Faction ties: `fac_pandora_court`, emergent ties to `fac_water_palace_order` through magical discipline, adversarial pressure from `fac_empire_court`
- Public persona: bright, stubborn, and socially mobile despite being boxed in by court expectation
- Internal motive: reclaim agency over her life, her future, and the story other people are trying to write for her
- Fear: becoming a diplomatic object rather than a person
- Contradiction: she resists control, but she is also deeply capable of taking on the same controlling habits when frightened
- Relationship map: daughter of Regan, tied to Dyluck by love and loss, challenged by Voss and Seremne in different ways, becomes the first party member who understands how institutions shape personal choice
- Act function: gives the story voice, political pressure, and emotional consequence
- Faction leverage: court knowledge, social access, magical affinity, soft-power mobility
- Visual design cues: ceremonial elegance with room for motion and combat, hair and accessories that reflect court identity without locking her into it
- Original Secret of Mana: primarily a rescue and romance axis around the male lead
- Revisit change: she becomes an active political actor and not merely the saved princess; her arc should expose how court systems trap women into symbolic roles
- Series input: broader Mana games often give magic users a larger civic or spiritual function; Primm should inherit that sense of responsibility
- Temple implication: she should be one of the first people to notice that temple orders are not neutral guardians but political actors with doctrine

## Character: Popoi

- ID: `char_popoi`
- Role: lost mana child, outsider witness, and the story’s memory fracture
- Faction ties: `fac_gemma_lineage`, fractured links to `fac_tree_palace_order`, endangered relation to ancient ruin networks
- Public persona: playful, abrasive, and eerily detached in the way someone becomes when history has already been taken from them
- Internal motive: recover identity and origin without surrendering the freedom that came from being unmoored
- Fear: that belonging will require obedience to a past that already failed
- Contradiction: acts like a trickster, but carries one of the deepest losses in the cast
- Relationship map: bonded to Randi and Primm through necessity, wary of temple authority, closest to the hidden truth of the Mana conflict, often the first to react when an area feels spiritually wrong
- Act function: reveals the scale of forgotten history and keeps the story from collapsing into human-only politics
- Faction leverage: mana perception, ancient memory resonance, elemental affinity, traversal through spaces others cannot read
- Visual design cues: small silhouette with uncanny detail, less "cute mascot" and more "survivor shaped by myth"
- Original Secret of Mana: comic relief and magical companion
- Revisit change: treat Popoi as a displaced survivor of a ruined order, with emotional weight and historical testimony behind the humor
- Series input: classic Mana stories often use childlike figures as custodians of old world truth; this version should lean into that without flattening them into exposition
- Temple implication: Popoi should sense when a temple’s public doctrine and hidden function diverge

## Character: Luka

- ID: `char_luka`
- Role: Water Palace authority, mentor, and first real interpreter of the new cosmology
- Faction ties: `fac_water_palace_order`, allied but not subordinate to `fac_gemma_lineage`
- Public persona: calm, intelligent guardian of a sacred institution
- Internal motive: keep the water tradition intact while deciding how much truth the world can survive at once
- Fear: that knowledge shared too early will accelerate corruption rather than prevent it
- Contradiction: she is a keeper of truth, but she withholds truth as a matter of governance
- Relationship map: guides the party into the larger world, respects Randi’s potential, understands Primm’s political pressure, and sees the Empire as a looming abstraction until it becomes immediate
- Act function: opens the temple logic of the story and converts myth into usable knowledge
- Faction leverage: ritual authority, archive access, purification rites, temple infrastructure
- Visual design cues: composed, aquatic, and unmistakably institutional; her design should imply stewardship rather than monarchy
- Original Secret of Mana: wise temple guide who explains what the player needs to know
- Revisit change: she becomes a full institutional character with limits, secrecy, and political judgment, not just a quest giver
- Series input: broader Mana temple figures often carry a mix of spiritual authority and administrative burden; Luka should embody both
- Temple implication: the Water Palace should feel like a living archive with competing duties, not a neutral tutorial chamber

## Character: Jema

- ID: `char_jema`
- Role: Gemma elder, last-link historian, and practical guardian of the old compact
- Faction ties: `fac_gemma_lineage`, ancient ties to multiple temple orders, trust relationship with `fac_potos_village`
- Public persona: patient village elder who knows more than he initially admits
- Internal motive: preserve the chance of restoration without forcing the world into a dead past
- Fear: that the Gemma legacy has become ceremonial while the real war advances elsewhere
- Contradiction: he exists to maintain continuity, yet continuity itself may be the problem
- Relationship map: mentor to Randi, intermittently in contact with temple authority, burdened by what he knows about the Mana Sword and why the old world collapsed, often forced to speak in fragments
- Act function: connects local survival to ancient catastrophe and gives the story moral memory
- Faction leverage: lineage authority, oral history, knowledge of hidden routes and old oaths
- Visual design cues: understated, weathered, practical, with the sense that every object he carries has been carried too long
- Original Secret of Mana: the friendly village elder who explains the quest
- Revisit change: he becomes a much more ambivalent custodian of history, including failures, omissions, and strategic silences
- Series input: classic Mana elders often carry the "what really happened" function; Jema should do this while also revealing the cost of incomplete stewardship
- Temple implication: Jema can identify when temples have drifted from original function into factional doctrine

## Character: Thanatos

- ID: `char_thanatos`
- Role: spiritual antagonist, manipulator of bodies and institutions, and late-story ideological pressure
- Faction ties: covertly adjacent to `fac_empire_mana_engineering_bureau`, opportunistic use of `fac_empire_unification_doctrine`, parasitic relation to broken temple systems
- Public persona: the story’s least honest intellect, presenting as inevitability, advisor, or necessity depending on context
- Internal motive: turn grief, fear, and disorder into controllable systems that serve his own permanence
- Fear: a world that chooses repair over dependency
- Contradiction: he speaks in the language of salvation while treating people as containers, inputs, and residues
- Relationship map: exploits imperial ambition, studies temple failures, sees the Mana Sword as a structural threat rather than a mere hero object, should be treated as present long before his full reveal
- Act function: reframes the conflict from simple conquest to the corruption of meaning itself
- Faction leverage: possession, forbidden research, institutional weakness, psychological leverage, magical contamination
- Visual design cues: elegant but wrong, always slightly too still, with visual language that suggests intelligence wearing human shape
- Original Secret of Mana: late-game hidden villain
- Revisit change: seed him earlier as a recurring pressure on institutions, so the endgame feels earned rather than abruptly revealed
- Series input: Mana antagonists often embody broken stewardship or misuse of old power; Thanatos should feel like the extreme endpoint of that pattern
- Temple implication: he is strongest where sacred systems are already split between public doctrine and hidden function

## Story and Setting Implications

- Randi, Primm, and Popoi should each represent a different relationship to inheritance: destiny, institution, and memory-loss.
- Luka and Jema should each represent a different relationship to sacred continuity: governance and stewardship.
- Thanatos should not feel like a separate final boss lane; he should be the parasitic logic that grows inside broken systems across the story.
- The temples should behave like Bethesda-style sacred institutions in the sense that they change over time, reveal layered histories, and expose competing factions, rather than staying fixed as simple dungeon checkpoints.
- The broader Mana-series influence should remain visible through relic culture, elemental identity, old-world fragmentation, and the sense that the world is larger than one kingdom or one sword.
- The original game’s clarity should remain recognizable, but the revisit should add politics, memory, institutional failure, and active temple transformation.
