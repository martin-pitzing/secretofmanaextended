# Moon Palace Dossier

- Temple ID: `temple_moon_palace`
- Scope: second-half dossier
- Labels used:
  - `Original anchor`: material already present in *Secret of Mana*
  - `Added series input`: motif or structure lifted from classic Mana-series feeling
  - `Added temple turn`: new twist for this revisit

## Original anchor

- Late-game sacred site.
- Tied to dreams, illusion, and emotional uncertainty.
- Functions as one of the final elemental thresholds before the world-scale collapse.

## Added series input

- Treat the site like a Mana-era memory vault, not only a dream palace.
- Make its rooms feel assembled from ritual, grief, and old pilgrimage use.
- Let the palace imply that sacred places can preserve feelings as well as facts.

## Added temple turn

- The Moon Palace stores curated dream-records and soft lies used by rulers, priests, and families who could not survive the unfiltered truth.
- Its keepers are not simple deceivers; they are custodians of emotional triage who decide when grief becomes too dangerous to release at once.
- The temple reveal is that comfort itself has been weaponized as a governing tool.
- Primm's arc sharpens here because the palace can stage a version of Dyluck that is tender, false, or both, forcing her to choose between consolation and truth.

## Local community

- Border villages and night-traveling pilgrims rely on Moon rites to manage mourning, sleep disturbance, and prophetic fever.
- A small class of shrine recorders translates dream reports into practical guidance for farmers, couriers, and healers.
- Families send offerings not only for blessings, but to keep private losses from becoming public collapse.

## Keeper / order

- The Moon Keepers present themselves as attendants of balance between rest and revelation.
- Their internal split is between:
  - custodians who believe selective concealment prevents social ruin
  - reformers who believe the temple has crossed into manipulation
- Their authority depends on trust, which makes the temple politically fragile once its methods are exposed.

## Surface quest

- Recover the missing dream-seal that stabilizes the palace's outer warding.
- Navigate a sequence of guided visions that appear to show the party their most desired outcomes.
- Decide whether to preserve a comforting illusion long enough to finish the ritual, or break it early and endure the immediate fallout.

## Hidden history

- The palace was used to soften defeat after an older Mana crisis by blending truth, sleep, and state ritual.
- Several public grief narratives in nearby regions were edited here to prevent panic and rebellion.
- One sealed chamber contains testimony showing that a prior authority chose emotional management over disclosure, creating a tradition of benevolent censorship.

## Party focus

- Primm is the primary pressure point because the temple forces her to confront longing, duty, and the shape of a truth she still wants.
- Popoi feels the site as an unstable memory field and risks bleed-through from fragmented ancestral impressions.
- Randi learns that protection can become another form of control when used without consent.

## Region state change

- Dreams in nearby settlements become vivid, repetitive, and harder to dismiss after restoration.
- Local healers gain access to better dream-reading and sleep remedies.
- Public faith in the Moon Keepers splits between gratitude and suspicion.
- One hidden grievance becomes public as a side effect of restored dream flow.

## Art signatures

- Silver-blue stone, pale lacquer, and reflective inlays.
- Hanging veils, moon pools, and suspended lantern constellations.
- Mirror corridors with distorted depth cues.
- Ritual sleep chambers with soft bedding and severe geometry.
- VFX language: drifting motes, glassy reflections, and overlapping silhouette echoes.

- **Added temple turn: the keepers are morally complicated custodians of emotional truth, not generic mystics.**
- **Added temple turn: the temple's resolution creates a visible social aftereffect in the region.**
