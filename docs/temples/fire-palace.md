# Temple Dossier: Fire Palace

- Temple ID: `temple_fire_palace`
- Region: Furnace temple, volcanic routes, and industrial borderland

## Original Anchor

- Salamando's domain
- elemental progression through fire alignment
- one of the major sacred sites that advances the party's magic and region access

## Added Changes From Original

- **Added temple turn: the keepers have been feeding outsiders false maps for years to keep anyone from reaching the true furnace heart.**
- **Added temple turn: the false maps look like cowardice or corruption until the buried disaster history is uncovered.**
- **Added temple turn: the furnace heart contains a sealed surge chamber that once burned a settlement off the map.**
- **Added temple turn: local authority is morally compromised in order to prevent a larger catastrophe.**

## Local Community

- Smiths, kiln workers, charcoal carriers, and border traders depend on stable fire flow for survival.
- Small settlement clusters around the temple rely on controlled heat for tools, medicines, and seasonal work.
- The region's economy is visibly tied to whether the temple can keep the furnace regulated.

## Keeper / Order

- Keeper: Fire Palace keepers and Salamando-linked ritual wardens
- Order: furnace wardens, map-keepers, and disaster-prevention custodians
- Public role: maintain the fire current, keep outsiders moving safely, and limit access to the true heart
- Hidden strain: the order lies by design because the real history is too dangerous to expose casually

## Surface Quest

- Follow the false maps, survive the outer heat routes, and restore the Fire Seed without triggering the buried surge chamber.
- The surface quest feels like a routing and survival problem before it becomes a moral one.

## Hidden History

- The palace once contained an uncontrolled fire event severe enough to erase a settlement and redefine local memory.
- The keepers learned that concealment can be a form of stewardship when the alternative is a second disaster.
- Imperial and mercantile interests now want the temple reopened for control, trade, and extraction rather than reverence.

## Party Focus

- Primm sees a version of authority that lies for the public good, forcing her to compare it with court secrecy and her own obligations.
- Randi confronts the idea that elemental power can be both necessary and catastrophic.
- The party learns that not every falsehood is simple betrayal, but every necessary lie still has a cost.

## Region State Change

- Fire magic becomes stronger and less predictable in the surrounding region.
- Local smithing, kiln work, and craft production revive.
- Military and logistical interest increases because the temple now looks like a strategic resource again.

## Art Signatures

- black basalt, ember seams, brass fittings, and heat shimmer layers
- false-route signage, hazard markers, and warded threshold gates
- the true furnace heart should read as beautiful, ancient, and frightening at the same time
- strong orange-red highlights against soot-dark stone, with glow visibility pushed through smoke and ash
