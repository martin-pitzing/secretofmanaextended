# Temple Dossier: Water Palace

- Temple ID: `temple_water_palace`
- Region: Sacred riverhead and central Water Palace basin

## Original Anchor

- Luka's seat of authority
- first explanation of the Mana Sword, seeds, and sacred duty
- the story's first major temple and the party's first contact with organized mana stewardship

## Added Changes From Original

- **Added temple turn: the palace archive is partially sealed from its own attendants.**
- **Added temple turn: the hidden records show earlier Gemma figures cut local temple orders out of the decision chain.**
- **Added temple turn: Luka is truthful, but she is also choosing stability over full disclosure.**
- **Added temple turn: restoration here is not only about power returning; it is about whether truth can survive restoration.**

## Local Community

- River farmers, ferrymen, and shrine workers depend on the palace for clean water, navigation, and seasonal rites.
- Lower-town families treat the palace as a blessing and a gatekeeper, not just a holy site.
- A small circle of clerks, attendants, and ritual cleaners knows the archive has grown unusually restricted.

## Keeper / Order

- Keeper: Luka
- Order: Water Palace attendants and archival ritual staff
- Public role: protect the source, guide the first awakening, and preserve ritual stability
- Hidden strain: attendants suspect the archive contains testimony about an older betrayal, but they are not cleared to read it

## Surface Quest

- Restore the first Water Seed, prove the sword's legitimacy, and secure passage into the next stretch of the journey.
- On the surface, the quest looks like a straightforward awakening rite interrupted by monster pressure and political delay.

## Hidden History

- The Water Palace once held the clearest records of the Gemma line's early concealment choices.
- A pre-imperial conflict around water access led to records being partitioned so local orders would not challenge the wider sacred chain.
- Luka knows that reopening the archive too early could destabilize both public trust and temple authority.

## Party Focus

- Randi learns that sacred authority can be protective and withholding at the same time.
- Primm sees how court power and temple power use different language to manage the same problem: who gets to know the truth.
- Popoi is affected by the archive's memory logic and begins to feel that Mana remembers more than people admit.

## Region State Change

- Water quality improves and route access stabilizes around the basin.
- Rumors spread that the Water Palace has records of older wars and older compromises.
- Public trust rises among communities dependent on the river, but some locals now fear the palace is hiding history on purpose.

## Art Signatures

- Blue-white stone with deep river reflections and layered archive chambers
- sealed shelves, water mirrors, and ritual channels integrated into the floor
- attendants in practical sacred robes rather than ornate court dress
- soft refraction lighting, floating droplets, and visual cues that the palace is a memory vessel as much as a temple
