# Temple Dossier: Wind Temple / Sky Route Sanctuary

- Temple ID: `temple_wind_sanctuary`
- Region: Wind sanctuary, aerial pilgrimage route, and storm diversion line

## Original Anchor

- wind element as mobility and openness
- an elemental sacred site that broadens traversal and magic progression
- a high-route temple tied to the sky and to movement across the world

## Added Changes From Original

- **Added temple turn: the sanctuary is maintained by aerial pilgrims who surrender personal memories to keep storm routes diverted away from settlements.**
- **Added temple turn: protection has been purchased through forgetting.**
- **Added temple turn: Popoi experiences memory bleed here and realizes that the region's safety was built on a hidden cost.**
- **Added temple turn: restoring the wind current may also release the memories the sanctuary has been carrying for others.**

## Local Community

- Aerial pilgrims, rope-route guides, and cliffside caretakers maintain the sanctuary and its storm paths.
- Nearby ridge settlements depend on the sanctuary's weather work to keep roads, farms, and messenger lines open.
- The local community treats memory loss as a sacred labor, not an accident.

## Keeper / Order

- Keeper: Waykeeper Ilyra Sen, wind pilots, pilgrims, and route sanctifiers
- Order: sky-route custodians and memory-bearing pilgrims
- Public role: preserve safe wind lanes, keep storms away from villages, and maintain the ritual balance of the high paths
- Hidden strain: the order has normalized forgetting as a price of protection

## Surface Quest

- Restore the Wind Seed, reopen safe travel paths, and clear the sanctuary's storm interference.
- The visible quest is about mobility and access, but the route is also a trial of memory and consent.

## Hidden History

- The sanctuary has repeatedly redirected storms by taking memories from those who live and travel there.
- The people who gave up memory did so to protect surrounding communities, which means the order cannot be dismissed as simple exploitation.
- The deeper question is whether a protective system is still just when its cost is hidden from the people it saves.

## Party Focus

- Popoi is the primary focus because the sanctuary's memory logic resonates with lost identity and unstable recall.
- Randi sees that travel, freedom, and protection can be in conflict when routes are maintained by sacrifice.
- Primm begins to understand that a good cause can still conceal coercive structure.

## Region State Change

- New flight paths, bridges, and storm-clear routes open.
- Messenger lines and trade movement improve.
- Some nearby families recover fragments of long-lost history, which creates both healing and conflict.

## Art Signatures

- pale sky stone, wind-cut platforms, suspended ropes, and thin cloud bridges
- memorial chimes, route markers, and memory tokens woven into shrine fixtures
- shifting cloud shadows and strong vertical composition to emphasize height and risk
- the sanctuary should feel open, airy, and slightly uncanny, as if the sky itself is holding testimony
