# Light Palace Dossier

- Temple ID: `temple_light_palace`
- Scope: second-half dossier
- Labels used:
  - `Original anchor`: material already present in *Secret of Mana*
  - `Added series input`: motif or structure lifted from classic Mana-series feeling
  - `Added temple turn`: new twist for this revisit

## Original anchor

- Light-aligned late sacred site.
- Associated with higher authority, revelation, and final-stage sacred escalation.
- Part of the path toward the endgame divine confrontation.

## Added series input

- Treat the site as a place where ritual, calendar, and legitimacy overlap.
- Let it feel older than any one kingdom and more administrative than expected.
- Pull in the Mana-series sense that sacred infrastructure can be civic as well as holy.

## Added temple turn

- The Light Palace once standardized public ritual across multiple kingdoms, making it part cathedral, part observatory, and part political archive.
- Its hidden machinery can validate rulers, omens, and prophecy claims, so control of interpretation matters as much as control of the seed.
- Pandora and Tasnica both have reason to compete over what the palace declares legitimate.
- The reveal is that sacred truth has always been administered through institutions that can be audited, manipulated, or captured.

## Local community

- Astrologers, census scribes, rite-captains, and boundary wardens maintain the temple's outer routines.
- Market towns nearby depend on the Light Palace for seasonal calendars, harvest timing, and formal blessing days.
- Visiting envoys treat the site as neutral ground until they realize it is also a power center.

## Keeper / order

- The Light Keepers are a disciplined order of ritual administrators and interpreters.
- Their split is between:
  - literalists who believe legitimacy must be certified through fixed rites
  - stewards who want the temple to remain a bridge between states instead of a throne-maker
- They are vulnerable to capture because whoever controls the reading of signs controls public confidence.

## Surface quest

- Recalibrate the temple's rite-lenses to align with a broken celestial schedule.
- Recover a lost registrar key that proves an old proclamation was altered.
- Escort the party through a public ritual that exposes how much of the palace's authority is performed rather than innate.

## Hidden history

- The Light Palace once helped normalize a compromise between rival kingdoms after a Mana crisis, but the compromise was rewritten as a timeless law.
- Several royal lineages gained legitimacy here through selective interpretation of prophecy.
- A concealed observatory annex proves the order has been editing celestial records whenever political stability required it.

## Party focus

- Jema is the main perspective pressure because this temple challenges what a sacred tradition protects when it claims to protect everyone.
- Primm sees how authority can be elegant, necessary, and dishonest at the same time.
- Randi learns that holy legitimacy can be manufactured without the people noticing the seam.

## Region state change

- Public ritual becomes more formal and less easily manipulated in nearby towns.
- Rival states intensify their claims to being the rightful interpreters of omen law.
- More citizens begin questioning whether blessing, law, and rule are actually separate things.
- A dormant civic dispute becomes active once the temple's records are exposed.

## Art signatures

- Warm white stone, gold filigree, and prism glass.
- Observatory domes, tall rectilinear halls, and sunline floors.
- Clerical desks, registrar shelves, and civic banners beside altar geometry.
- VFX language: hard shafts of light, spectral annotation marks, and lens flare through crystal apertures.
