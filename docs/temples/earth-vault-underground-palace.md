# Temple Dossier: Earth Vault / Underground Palace

- Temple ID: `temple_earth_vault`
- Region: Underground palace, excavation strata, and dwarven salvage corridors

## Original Anchor

- early temple progression
- connection to dwarves, ruins, and buried power
- one of the first places where the party feels the world's old infrastructure beneath the surface

## Added Changes From Original

- **Added temple turn: the temple is built above a sealed extraction complex from an older Mana war.**
- **Added temple turn: dwarven salvage clans and the Earth Vault keepers disagree over whether the site is a tomb, a machine, or a duty.**
- **Added temple turn: restoring the seed stabilizes the region but also reactivates old conduits that imperial engineers can detect.**
- **Added temple turn: the region now changes because the temple is part shrine, part sealed industrial memory, part contested heritage.**

## Local Community

- Dwarven salvage clans work the upper tunnels, sorting relics, ore, and collapsed stone.
- Nearby surface settlements rely on the vault for structural stone, tools, and access to older passages.
- Labor crews, cart-haulers, and shrine wardens all treat the site as economically necessary even when they disagree about its meaning.

## Keeper / Order

- Keeper: Earth Vault custodians and dwarven oversight stewards
- Order: Earth Vault wardens, excavation priests, and salvage clan delegates
- Public role: preserve the seal, regulate access, and keep the region from collapsing under its own history
- Hidden strain: one faction wants to keep the vault closed forever, while another believes only managed excavation can keep the complex safe

## Surface Quest

- Restore the Earth Seed and clear the route through the underground strata to stabilize the region.
- The visible quest is about clearing a blockage, reclaiming a sacred chamber, and surviving the deep route's hostile pressure.

## Hidden History

- The Earth Vault sits on top of a failed Mana-era extraction system built to draw strength from the earth faster than the world could recover.
- The old machinery still remembers how to respond to mana flow, which is why restoration also wakes dormant conduits.
- The vault's keepers inherited a duty they did not build, while the salvage clans inherited damage they did not cause.

## Party Focus

- Randi learns that buried power is never just buried; it shapes the present through what can still be mined and repaired.
- Jema's Gemma history matters here because old concealment decisions are tied to the vault's sealing.
- Watts becomes more important because weapon restoration, salvage ethics, and temple awakening are materially linked.

## Region State Change

- Deeper chambers open to dwarven access under stricter oversight.
- Imperial interest increases because old conduits and extraction traces become detectable again.
- Local industry improves, but so does pressure to turn sacred ruin into strategic infrastructure.

## Art Signatures

- layered stonework, support beams, lantern chains, and mineral seams that glow only where mana still moves
- a visible contrast between ceremonial chambers and rough salvage tunnels
- old machine geometry partly swallowed by rock and shrine markers
- heavy reds, iron grays, and ochres with gold-lit mineral veins
