# Secret of Mana Revisited: Magic Doctrine

## Purpose

This document defines the language and structure of magic before gameplay systems are implemented.

Use these labels:

- `Original anchor`: material already visible in *Secret of Mana*
- `Added revisit change`: new structure or terminology for this project

## Core Rule

Magic is not one thing.

It is a set of relationships between:

- Mana in the world
- spirits and temple orders
- trained users
- relic devices
- memory and sacrifice

## Marked Changes From Original Secret Of Mana

- **Original anchor: elemental spirits grant spells and the Mana Sword channels sacred power.**
- **Added revisit change: magic is divided by doctrine and method, not only by element.**
- **Added revisit change: different factions teach different acceptable uses of Mana.**
- **Added revisit change: weapon restoration, temple restoration, and forbidden mana engineering are part of one continuum.**

## The Four Doctrines Of Magic

### 1. Pact Magic

- `Original anchor`: elemental spirits and temple bonds
- `Added revisit change`: this is the cleanest accepted form of magic, based on covenant, taboo, and reciprocal duty
- common users: temple keepers, selected travelers, spirit-attuned mages
- narrative use: magic as relationship and worldview

### 2. Rite Magic

- `Added revisit change`: long-form ceremonial magic using location, timing, chorus, tools, and preparation
- common users: temple orders, funeral singers, court ritualists, archive keepers
- narrative use: public healing, warding, weather management, memory binding, legitimacy rituals

### 3. Field Magic

- `Original anchor`: combat magic used by the party and enemies
- `Added revisit change`: the practical battle and traversal form of magic derived from pact or rite traditions
- common users: adventurers, officers, trained mages, shrine defenders
- narrative use: what most characters call "magic" in everyday language

### 4. Engineered Mana Abuse

- `Original anchor`: ancient Mana Fortress abuse and imperial hunger for old power
- `Added revisit change`: artificial extraction, redirection, storage, weaponization, and experimental recombination of Mana without reciprocal stewardship
- common users: Mana Engineering Bureau, old war projects, corrupted labs, some hidden cult cells
- narrative use: the line between civilization-saving utility and catastrophic control

## The Six Working Terms To Use In Writing

### Attunement

- how deeply a person or place is aligned with an element, spirit, or sacred route

### Saturation

- how much active Mana pressure a body, object, or region is holding

### Corruption

- destabilized Mana acting on life, matter, or memory

### Hollowing

- a loss-state where memory, will, or emotional integrity has been eroded by misuse of Mana

### Conduit

- any natural or built channel through which Mana is directed

### Seal Debt

- the cost deferred when a dangerous site is sealed instead of healed

## Weapon Schools

These are not yet combat mechanics. They are world and design categories that later combat can build from.

### Blade School

- associated with judgment, cutting falsehood, and direct hero-symbolism
- strongest tie to the Mana Sword and Gemma tradition

### Polearm School

- associated with reach, warding lines, and defensive formation
- often used by shrine guards and organized militaries

### Chain And Flail School

- associated with disruption, capture, and anti-creature control
- useful in temple or ruin interiors where raw killing force is not always the goal

### Bow School

- associated with distance, omen reading, and patient action
- often tied to watchers, route keepers, and forest shrine defenders

### Hammer And Axe School

- associated with material force, salvage, and breaking sealed matter
- strongest cultural tie to dwarven and labor traditions

### Spear Of Light / Staff Lines

- associated with channeling, rite support, and ritual geometry
- useful bridge between combat and sacred practice

## Elemental Philosophy

Each element should mean more than its spell animation.

- water: continuity, memory, reflection, mercy
- earth: custody, burden, endurance, burial
- fire: transformation, appetite, renewal, catastrophe
- wind: change, loss, route, release
- light: order, recognition, authority, revelation
- dark: secrecy, grief, necessity, compromise
- moon: dream, desire, illusion, emotional truth
- wood or tree-aligned mana: continuity of life, sacrifice, inheritance, regrowth

## Faction Uses Of Magic

### Water Palace

- prefers pact and rite magic
- suspicious of engineered channels unless absolutely necessary

### Pandora Court

- uses rite magic for legitimacy and healing, but often treats it instrumentally

### Empire

- tolerates pact magic
- prizes engineered mana abuse because it can be standardized and controlled

### Thanatos Network

- exploits corruption, hollowing, grief rites, and illicit conduits

### Dwarven Forges

- sit between material craft and sacred restoration
- understand that weapons are also memory-bearing conduits
