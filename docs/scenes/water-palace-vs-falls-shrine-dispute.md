# Scene Brief: Water Palace vs Falls Shrine Dispute

## Purpose

This is the first explicit scene where temple authority disagrees on-screen rather than only in lore.

It is designed to pay off the ideological split already established in the faction bible and temple documents.

## Scene ID

- `sc_pre_ch03_water_vs_falls_dispute`

## Placement

- best inserted between late Chapter 2 fallout and early Chapter 3 travel
- can also function as an interlude scene triggered when new archive material reaches the falls region

## Participants

- Luka
- Archivist Neral
- Sister Yselle of the Falls Shrines
- Randi
- optionally Primm as the sharpest political observer in the room

## Original Anchor

- Luka is the first temple authority in *Secret of Mana*.
- Local shrine structures near Potos are implied but not dramatized as institutions in conflict.

## Added Revisit Change

- Sister Yselle directly accuses the Water Palace of centralizing truth while villages carry the cost of concealment.
- Luka argues that partial truth kept the world from fracturing further.
- Randi is forced to hear that his own story began inside a system both sides believe mishandled its duty.

## Scene Thesis

The sacred orders did not fail because they had no principles. They failed because each principle protected one part of the world by burdening another.

## Player Goal

- witness the dispute
- understand the ideological split
- leave with a clearer sense that temple restoration is politically charged

## Narrative Beat

- Yselle says the falls were never just blessed ground; they were a containment wound rural people were ordered to guard without explanation.
- Luka counters that broader disclosure during earlier crises would have invited imperialization, panic, and temple fracture.
- Neral introduces archive fragments showing the Gemma line brokered the compromise.
- Randi realizes his exile was the downstream effect of a much older sacred decision.

## Gameplay Beat

- dialogue scene with optional follow-up questions
- no combat
- choice-flavored responses from Randi and Primm, but same canonical outcome

## Exit Condition

- Yselle leaves unconvinced but no longer fully outside the conversation
- Luka instructs the party to keep moving before the Empire reaches the same conclusions
- Randi carries a sharper burden: he is not correcting a single mistake, but inheriting a long structure of managed failure

## Resulting Flags

- `flag_faction_water_palace_doctrine_contested`
- `flag_region_falls_shrine_grievance_exposed`
- `flag_character_randi_containment_truth_learned`

## Why This Scene Matters

- it makes temple ideology dramatic, not abstract
- it strengthens Potos retroactively
- it gives Luka needed friction before she calcifies into pure mentor authority
- it prepares the player to trust temple keepers less simply, which is necessary for the later palace turns
