# Secret of Mana Revisited: Visual Production Decisions

## Purpose

This document locks the first visual production decisions that would otherwise keep the art pipeline vague.

## Decision 1: First Benchmark Environment

- locked choice: `Water Palace inner chamber`
- secondary benchmark after that: `Potos falls and seal site`

## Why Water Palace First

- it carries theology, archive politics, and the first major ritual scene
- it tests the exact balance this project needs: sacred beauty, institutional strain, and detailed pixel readability
- it is more representative of the revisit's unique identity than a pure village or field space
- it can validate dialogue staging, lighting, props, and mana VFX in one room

## What The Benchmark Must Prove

- layered semi-overhead 2D environment readability
- ritual interaction staging
- dialogue scene composition
- palette discipline for sacred spaces
- mana glow treatment without remake-style gloss
- clear hero route and interaction read under the action-RPG camera lane
- prop density for archive, temple, and water-channel storytelling

## Benchmark Room Lock

- benchmark room: `Water Palace inner chamber`
- supporting adjacent space: short exterior causeway or palace approach
- no combat required in the first benchmark pass
- one optional ritual activation VFX pass is required

## Decision 2: Dialogue Portrait Format

- locked choice: `detailed pixel portrait panels`
- rejected option: `lower-resolution sprite busts only`

## Portrait Format Spec

- visible portrait target: `96x96`
- style: hand-authored pixel bust portraits, not painted illustrations and not cropped gameplay sprites
- framing: shoulders-up or chest-up, slight 3/4 angle by default
- expression counts are now locked by tier in `docs/art/portrait-batch-01-spec.md`

## Why This Format

- the project is aiming for a more detailed pixel identity, so portraits should carry emotional nuance that the gameplay sprites cannot
- `96x96` is detailed enough for personality without overwhelming a `480x270` internal resolution
- lower-resolution sprite busts would undersell court scenes, temple politics, and late-game emotional revelations

## Style Rules For Portraits

- pixel-authored only
- no smooth-shaded remake look
- lighting should follow regional color scripts
- portraits should show fabric class, travel wear, and regional identity clearly
- major authority figures should read immediately through silhouette, trim, and bearing
