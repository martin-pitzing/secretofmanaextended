# Secret of Mana Revisited: Art Direction Brief

## Creative Target

Make the game feel like a lost, polished 2D action RPG reinterpretation of *Secret of Mana*, not a 3D remake and not a minimal retro throwback.

The art should communicate:

- wonder
- sacred decay
- lush regional identity
- hidden imperial machinery
- mana as a beautiful but unstable force

## Style Statement

This project should look like **ornate pixel fantasy with strong material storytelling and clear action-RPG readability**.

That means:

- dense foliage, carved stone, ritual metals, woven fabrics
- layered foreground and background depth
- color-scripted regions
- expressive sprite acting
- readable but elaborate spell geometry

It should not look like:

- toy-like 3D anime characters
- flat RPG Maker nostalgia
- blurry HD-2D bloom overload
- low-detail mobile pixel art

## Reference Lane

The visual target is not pure nostalgia and not pure imitation.

Use this lane:

- `Secret of Mana` for party-forward sacred fantasy, radial-menu identity, and regional tone
- `Alundra` for jump-capable traversal, room-scale action staging, and dense 2D adventure-space reads
- `Terranigma` for brisk hero movement, run-attack-jump verbs, and SNES-lineage combat rhythm

The correct visual short-hand is:

- `polished 2D Mana-lineage action RPG`

Do not reduce it to:

- `top-down pixel remake`
- `just Secret of Mana but prettier`

## Action-RPG Read Priorities

No matter how attractive a scene is, it still has to read as an action RPG.

Prioritize:

- readable hero locomotion
- committed attack animations
- enemy anticipation and recovery
- spatial combat lanes
- damage feedback
- camera support for combat
- ability selection flow
- party-state visibility
- strong enemy silhouettes
- clear interaction between melee, magic, and movement

If these do not read clearly, the scene may still look beautiful, but it will read as an exploration prototype rather than an action RPG.

## Regional Art Modules

### Potos And The Falls

Mood:
- sheltered, humble, old fears under pastoral calm

Visual language:
- wet wood, rope bridges, mossy stone, carved village charms, waterfall mist

Color notes:
- soft greens, cedar browns, cool water highlights

### Water Palace

Mood:
- serene authority with visible strain

Visual language:
- pale stone, engraved channels, suspended braziers, reflective pools, worn sacred symbols

Color notes:
- ivory, turquoise, muted gold, blue-white mana accents

### Pandora And The Ruins

Mood:
- civic collapse under military pressure

Visual language:
- banners, barricades, sealed plazas, stained walls, half-abandoned noble interiors

Color notes:
- iron gray, faded crimson, sickly violet corruption highlights

### Gaia's Navel

Mood:
- industrious underground warmth above something ancient

Visual language:
- forge sparks, ore veins, carved tunnels, improvised lifts, excavated relic fragments

Color notes:
- charcoal, copper, ember orange, deep teal shadows

### Upper Land

Mood:
- dreamlike vitality with underlying instability

Visual language:
- oversized leaves, hanging roots, pollen haze, soft fungal light, vertical terrain

Color notes:
- saturated greens, lime glow, orchid accents, cool bark shadows

### Desert, Sandship, And Imperial Zones

Mood:
- extraction, movement, occupied trade routes

Visual language:
- sails, brass fittings, heat shimmer, checkpoints, supply crates, mana engines

Color notes:
- ochre, sand, brass, blackened steel, cyan instrument lights

### Tree Palace, Pure Land, Mana Fortress

Mood:
- holy memory giving way to sublime catastrophe

Visual language:
- giant roots, impossible white flora, fractured geometry, floating relics, exposed mana conduits

Color notes:
- alabaster, jade, rose-white, star-blue, deep void contrast

## Character Art Principles

- Characters need stronger silhouettes than the original SNES sprites while keeping pixel economy.
- Costumes should show culture, class, and travel wear.
- Each protagonist needs one strong shape cue:
  - Randi: practical hero silhouette, sword-forward, earth-tone traveler
  - Primm: noble tailoring reworked for travel, layered fabrics, healing motifs
  - Popoi: asymmetry, curious shapes, mana-touched color accents, outsider energy

## Animation Priorities

If production bandwidth is limited, spend it here first:

1. idle personality loops
2. run cycles
3. cast animations
4. hit and stagger reactions
5. weapon finishers
6. environmental ambient loops
7. boss telegraphs

## Mana Effect Language

Use a consistent visual grammar:

- water mana: rings, droplets, refraction
- earth mana: plates, runes, cracks, lifted debris
- fire mana: braided flame, sigils, pressure bursts
- wind mana: arcs, streamlines, drifting particles
- light mana: lattice, prisms, radial seals
- dark or corrupted mana: inversion, fracture, unstable edge noise

## Art Approval Checklist

- Does the scene read clearly at gameplay distance?
- Do hero movement, attack arcs, and enemy telegraphs still read clearly at gameplay distance?
- Does it still feel pixel-authored when zoomed in?
- Is there a strong regional identity?
- Are materials distinct without overtexturing?
- Does mana look sacred and unstable rather than generic magic?
- Could this be mistaken for the official remake?
  If yes, reject and restyle.
